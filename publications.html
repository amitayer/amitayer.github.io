A ruptured infectious intracranial aneurysm with a combined fungal and bacterial etiology.

We report on an immunocompetent patient who presented with a somewhat typical clinical picture of bacterial endocarditis found to have a ruptured 3 mm saccular aneurysm in the right anterior cerebral artery (ACA) at the bifurcation of A4 and A5 with associated subarachnoid hemorrhage (SAH). To our knowledge, this is the first reported case of a mycotic aneurysm with combined bacterial and fungal etiology.

Clin Neurol Neurosurg. 2013 Nov;115(11):2393-6. Abecassis IJ, Adel JG, Ayer A, Batjer HH. PMID: 24034820

IL-27 enhances LPS-induced proinflammatory cytokine production via upregulation of TLR4 expression and signaling in human monocytes.

To our knowledge, this study is the first to show a role for IL-27 in regulating TLR4 expression and function. This work is significant as it reveals new mechanisms by which IL-27 can enhance proinflammatory responses that can occur during bacterial infections.

The sociopolitical history and physiological underpinnings of skull deformation.

In this report, the evidence, mechanisms, and rationale for the practice of artificial cranial deformation (ACD) in ancient Peru and during Akhenaten's reign in the 18th dynasty in Egypt (1375-1358 BCE) are reviewed. Following the anthropological review, a neurosurgical focus is directed to instances of plagiocephaly in modern medicine, with special attention to the conditions' etiology, consequences, and treatment.

Neurosurg Focus. 2010 Dec;29(6):E1. Ayer A, Campbell A, Appelboom G, Hwang BY, McDowell M, Piazza M, Feldstein NA, Anderson RC. PMID: 21121715
